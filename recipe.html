Idaho roasted potato recipe

The first potatoes grown in Idaho were planted in northern Idaho by Rev. Henry Spaulding. It was a successful crop but his missionary work was brought to an end by the Whitman massacre and the Spauldings were forced to leave in 1850. Potatoes again made their way to Idaho by way of Utah. Pioneers entered the Salt Lake Valley on July 22, 1847. On July 24 a five-acre potato patch was plowed and seed potatoes planted. The first irrigation in Salt Lake Valley was for the benefit of the newly planted potatoes. A week later the potatoes were growing.

the recipe!

  • 3 pounds, russet Idaho potatoes
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons of granulated garlic
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of pepper
  • 1-2 tablespoons of fresh rosemary (thyme if preferred)

    • the directions!

    1. First, preheat the oven to 425 degrees farenheit
    2. Salt water and put it on the stove to boil, then cut the potatoes into cubes about 1 inch in size (peeling optional)
    3. Cover the potatoes in Rosemary, salt, pepper and garlic in a largely sized bowl
    4. Line the pan with parchment paper and place the potatoes on the pan. Bake them for 25 minutes, flip them and then bake them for 20 more (golden brown color)
    5. Potaotes should be served hot, enjoy!
